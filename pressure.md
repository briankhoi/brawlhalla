# pressure

use neutral to gain information about the opponent. 

pressure is the act of going near your opponent and either pretending you'll hit them or actually doing so, and observing what they do in response.

some examples of doing this:
- empty hopping around them in their dead zones
- 

then, find ways to capitalize on that response. also note that player behavior will likely change and adapt throughout the course of the game, so it's useful to always pressure and verify if their habits are the same or if they start to change.

for example, if they always spot dodge when you approach, you can wait it out then punish. then, if they adapt and start to disrupt when you approach, you can bait it out and punish.

tangentially related, it's also useful to understand player tendencies/different playstyles.
- if opponent is really aggressive on the ground/attacking, they'll prob always do wakeups/disrupt and dodge in or down
- if they avoid your attacks, they'll prob dodge away or up away
-  if someone likes to cross up (i.e. jump over you), they usually like to dodge in/like stage control
- people will typically do the opposite of what they get punished for. so if they dodge in and you punish they might dodge away next time. another example is when edgeguarding, so if you do a grounded attack, they're much more likely to stay floaty when they get on stage, so you can spear nlight then mixup with sair

using these generalized playstyles, we can look out for these types of behavior during neutral and punish them accordingly

more tips for reading people:
- opt for more coverage than hard reads during exchanges (e.g. spear slight cc nair instead of slight predict in-dodge then dlight)
- empty hop after you hit an attack and then react; this lets you cover more dodges/movement and more consistently rather than just reading a specific dodge
- if they start to slow down, it could be a sign that they're starting to adapt, so you can try a different read than normal
- sometimes if they're really scared, they won't dodge since they're worried about giving away their dodge for free. so in that case it's option 3 aka they do nothing when you're the attacker so just do full string like spear slight dlight recv to kill
- if you know you can't punish, you have two options. first is to just reset back to neutral. second is to do something like dash cancel or jump fastfall to make it seem like you will punish to bait out their attack, then you punish

also note that you won't be able to pressure the same way all the time. so because of that it's important to always be mixing up how you approach people, for example maybe you backdodge or approach aerially or just straight up attacking them instead of delaying.

also learn why people panic the way they do.
