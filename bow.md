# bow
based on: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V1BHK6Fx2t4

bow has a lot of range, only thing preventing you from taking over is your assertion on the ground. so, bow's main objective is maintaining center stage control and thus keeping your opponent away from center stage

to do this, anytime you get the opportunity, string nair into recovery or nlight to keep the opponent constantly in the air

when you go for a nair:
- try to string for it again 
- if they go to the side you go for recovery
- if they go to the side and you're grounded you can nlight and go for another attempted nair/recv

stay close to opponents to where you can either dash slight or just dlight to break neutral, then try to string them into the air

if you try to neutral break with your grounded options, and due to that pressure they use a resource like a jump, remember you always have dash jump sair or walk forward nlight to punish that. another extreme option is also nsig

in general, bow is a weapon where it can't really extend, but can cover a lot of different angles. so when engaging, if they have no dodge then you can follow up (e.g. slight dlight nair) and it's true, but otherwise you're just trying to read a dodge, which makes bow a very committal weapon. in addition, even when you do catch a dodge, since bow can't extend, you're only entitled to usually only one extra hit, otherwise you're greeding

for grounded attack follow ups (based on slight):
- slight walk forward nlight (and walk distance is based on whether you predict them to dodge in or away, if away then walk further before nlight). this covers all aggressive (in dodges) and neutral (neutral as in directly up/down/spot) aside from spot dodges
- if spot dodge, just wait and slight again into an actual string this time since they have no dodge
- if away dodge, just dash jump and sair, covers all up away, down away, and normal away - this option though is very, very committal so be careful when using

after these options you can also try to look for angles to rising dair (jump dair but dair as you are going up, rather than falling back down from jump), nlight, or another slight - try to make it so they are completely blocked of every option they have
- so like say you slight dash jump sair and they're off stage. if you predict they'll use a resource to go up, like a jump back on stage, you can nlight them

for aerial follow ups (namely sair):
- slightly jump forward, cc reverse nair, covers all aggressive/in dodges, spot and directly up dodge 
- for away dodges, you can either jump cc nair (no reverse, and nair when you go on top of them during your cc drift), or you can do above read but move forward a bit during your initial sair landing to offset the gap of their away dodge and the read above

to modify this for grounded dair (dair that hits the ground, so you also fall onto the ground during recovery frames) followups, it's the exact same thing but use forward momentum when landing. you can also adapt it to work for rising dair by doing no jump, just cc immediately and fall on top of them then when overlapped do a nair.
- if they aggressive dodge, you can also just dair, wait, nlight

juggling is the process of keeping opponents in the air, and punishing them trying to land. bow is exceptionally good at this, as we have a lot of attacks that send the opponent upward (nlight, recv, nair). 

generally, after you knock them in the air (via nair), you can:
- if you hit a nair on ground level, try to immediately jump nair and check them, incase they don't use resources
- if they do use a resource, then wait for them to exhaust it them punish as they are landing via nlight, recv, or nair

also important to note that when we juggle, we always try to keep them in the air as long as possible (as that is what juggling is), and as a result, all of our extended options are nlight, recv, or nair, as opposed to something like sair which would work (and does maintain stage control) but doesn't achieve juggling's goal

after juggling for a bit, your opponent now may be a bit scared to land, so they'll mix things up and use their options more in the air so they don't get hit. so as a result you should also mix up your options (e.g. if you condition them with nlight, instead surprise them with recv). also important to note here, in order for them to even react like this, you need to be actively creating the pressure, by staying near them while they land and threatening them with your attacks

- dash slight
- dlight
- if they're not in range of those two, you can dash jump rising sair
- if they anticipate the sair and are too close, you can rising dair
