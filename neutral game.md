# neutral game

based on sting ray guide: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rT1c6L_-hO4

terminology:
- advantage state: when you land a hit or force opponent into a bad position. you control the pace and look to string damage
- neutral state: even state where neither player has an advantage or disadvantage
- disadvantage state: when you get hit, lose stage control, or get forced offstage and consequently have limited options and/or are low on resources. goal here is to reset back to neutral

neutral game revolves around a finite state machine of the 3 states above, as well as two jobs: attacker and defender. these rotate and mainly are based off of who approaches first.

attacker's job:
- threaten defender with their hitboxes via:
	- movement sequence
	- speed
	- what options you have on your character/weapon
- understand what the defender wants

the types of attacks you have influence how your opponent reacts to threats. for example, if you're on bow you'll want to dlight, so your opponent will anticipate that and jump. so if you dash in/make it look like you'll dlight, you can bait out their jump

disrespecting vs. respecting:
- respect: when your opponent understand you have a specific option and that you will use it when necessary
- disrespect: when your opponent doesn't care that you have an option because they know that you are not going to use it

an example of this is you walking up to them with sword, if they get out of the way then they're respecting you

as an attacker, the defender's behavior will often fall into one of 3 states:
- defender will move away from your zones to respect you (respect)
	- win by understanding what attack you're threatening and where your opponent is trying to move to avoid that
	- after analyzing where they want to move, follow up and punish their reaction, so for example if you're on sword and dash in to nlight, and they jump or dash back, you can jump sair for first, or dash slight for second scenario
- defenders will attempt to attack you to disrupt your approach options (middle ground)
	- win this by dashing in then immediately back dashing (i.e. baiting out this behavior from them) and then dashing back in to punish while they're in recovery frames
	- this option isn't meant to be done frequently, but rather used as a mixin, so occasionally when approaching them, rather than attacking straight away, you instead just do the above to bait an attack then engage
	- another way to win this is by performing an attack before they do theirs/using an attack with higher priority, e.g. spear nlight or weapon toss
	- weapon throws primary purpose (at least on stage) is when you're too scared to approach but want to have a hitbox out/attack them
- defenders will stand still (disrespect)
	- win this by hitting them with the attack you were planning on hitting them with in the first place

defenders will avoid your attack based on:
- stage control
	- they can back up if they're in middle of stage, but if they're on edge that's one less option for them (so they're more inclined to jump or cross you to go center of stage)
- the types of attacks your character/weapon has
	- visualize your threat zones to see what your opponent is scared of and what they don't respect. example: ![[images/Pasted image 20260417223811.png]]
	- so notice when opponent is in those threat zones, and if they're just sitting or standing there, then they don't respect it and you can hit them
- their damage (i.e. if they're low, they may react more defensively than normal)

more attacker behavior:
- finding kills in neutral is same thing as playing neutral normally itself, except you just change the move you do. for example instead of bow dlight nlight to juggle, you can dlight recovery instead, or follow up on their backdash and use a sig
- your opponent will whiff. always critically think if you can punish it or not. 
	- if you know you can't punish it, don't, cause it could lead to a wakeup attack from them and instead result in a bad trade for you. essentially don't greed for punishes, be patient and rational
	- observe their behavior and analyze what they do after whiffing. they could either jump away or do a wakeup attack or something else, but the point is to analyze their behavior/dash in and out to see what they do, then punish it next time 
- when your opponent jumps, they either have to land right now or later. you want to observe to see how many resources (jumps/dodges/recv) they use before they land and other general patterns to punish accordingly. while they're in the air, you can just be patient and track them, while also making sure you're aware of your threat zone and theirs. they will land eventually, so once again be patient and wait for that moment. examples of patterns are:
	- them always burning a dodge before landing
	- landing with a falling sair 
	- immediately doing an attack after dodging
	- them always fast falling
	- sometimes if they're in the red, they might be more defensive and go back to opposite end of stage
- same attacker principles apply to in-air as well, like if you're both parallel and they do not respect you, you can just go for a sair




defender's job:
- successfully disrupt the attacker's approach and become the attacker

you do this by understanding timing and the approach options of the attacker:
- if they're the type of player who constantly spams attack/instantly attack when near you, you as the defender back away and avoid attacker's threat zones (and then punish)
- if they approach you and get in your threat zone and don't respect the fact that you have options that can hit them, hit them with that option
- always analyze what the opponent is doing. sometimes (like lowkey you on spear with slight), they don't use certain options. so if they show that they don't use the moves, then you can play a bit more leniently and step into those not-used moves' threat zone/collapse their options to better predict what they will do
